How to Use the Effects Pedals

Welcome to the audio effects pedal interface! Here's how to use it:

  • Choose File: Click the "Choose File" button to choose an audio file to play. Then click the "Play File" button to play the audio file.
  • Reverb Pedal: Adjust the "Effect Amount" and "Mix" knobs to control the reverb effect. Toggle the "On/Off" switch to enable or disable the effect.
  • Delay Pedal: Adjust the "Effect Amount" and "Mix" knobs to control the delay effect. Toggle the "On/Off" switch to enable or disable the effect.
  • Microphone Input: Click the "Enable Microphone" button to use your microphone as the audio source. Click the "Disable Microphone" button to stop using your microphone as the audio source.
  • Resume Audio: If your microphone input doesn't seem to be working, refresh the page and then click the "Resume Audio" button. You should now hear your microphone input.

Effects Pedal Documentation

Reverb Pedal

The Reverb Pedal simulates the natural reverberation of a physical space.

  • Effect Amount: Controls the intensity of the reverb effect.
  • Mix: Adjusts the balance between the dry (original) and wet (reverberated) signal.

Delay Pedal

The Delay Pedal creates echoes of the input signal.

  • Effect Amount: Controls the delay time (how long it takes for the echo to repeat).
  • Mix: Adjusts the balance between the dry (original) and wet (delayed) signal.
